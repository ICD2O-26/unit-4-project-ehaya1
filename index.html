Defining General Intelligence

Unlike "Narrow AI" (e.g., facial recognition or chess engines), AGI represents a system capable of transfer learning—the ability to take knowledge from one domain (like physics) and apply it to a completely different one (like political strategy) without human intervention.

Technical Architecture

Moving toward AGI requires a shift from Connectionist (neural net) approaches to Neuro-Symbolic hybrids that combine data-driven learning with hard logic.

The "Augmentation Gap"

Goldman Sachs Research (April 2024-2026) shows that while AI substitutes for 16,000 monthly payroll roles in the US, it augments 9,000 others. This 0.1% increase in unemployment marks the beginning of the "Intelligence Economy."

Canada's Digital Leadership

Canada is pioneering the Artificial Intelligence and Data Act (AIDA). This policy framework treats AGI as a "High-Impact" technology, requiring developers to maintain transparent "Kill Switch" logs and audit trails for autonomous agents.
