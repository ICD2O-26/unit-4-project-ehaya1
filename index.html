AGI.

Artificial General Intelligence

01. The Biological Mimicry

Unlike Narrow AI (which follows fixed rules), AGI is a hypothetical system that possesses Generalization Ability. This means it can transfer skills from one domain—like chemistry—to another, such as music theory, without being reprogrammed.

How it Roughly Works:

AGI relies on Recursive Self-Improvement. It uses Neural Networks to observe patterns, but adds a layer of Heuristic Reasoning, allowing it to solve "Unfamiliar Problems" it has never encountered before.

02. Social & Ethical Paradoxes

The Alignment Problem
This is the risk that an AGI's goals will not align with human values. As the philosopher Nick Bostrom notes, an AGI doesn't have to be "evil" to be dangerous; it just has to be "more competent than us at pursuing a goal that harms us by accident."
Economic Post-Scarcity
If AGI can perform any cognitive task, the traditional "job" disappears. While this could lead to a world without poverty (Post-Scarcity), it requires a total redesign of global finance, likely through Universal Basic Income (UBI).
Wait, what about the "Black Box"?

The "Black Box Problem" refers to the lack of transparency. As AGI creates its own logic pathways, humans may lose the ability to explain how the machine reached a conclusion, leading to an Interpretability Crisis.
