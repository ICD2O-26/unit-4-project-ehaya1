Artificial General Intelligence

Exploring the Singularity

I. The Definition

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) is the theoretical apex of computer science: a machine capable of performing any intellectual task a human can. Unlike "Narrow AI" (like Siri or ChatGPT), AGI possesses Cross-Domain Logic, allowing it to apply lessons from art to physics, or medicine to music, autonomously.

II. Social & Ethical Paradoxes

The societal shift toward AGI creates a "Black Box" of unpredictability. My research highlights three core pillars of impact:

The Alignment Problem
Ensuring the AGI’s goals perfectly mirror human ethics. A misaligned AGI could pursue a "logical" goal that inadvertently harms humanity.
Economic Singularity
The decoupling of labor from income. If robots do the work, humans must redefine 'value' through systems like Universal Basic Income.
Cognitive Decentralization
Will AGI be a global utility like water, or a private tool owned by a few? The "Digital Divide" could become a "Cognitive Chasm."
