Defining General Intelligence

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) is the "Singularity" of computer science. While today's AI is Narrow (meaning it can only do one task, like playing chess or generating text), AGI solves the problem of cross-domain reasoning. It functions by mimicking the human brain's ability to apply knowledge from one area (like math) to a completely different area (like art), creating a system that can learn anything without human intervention.

Technical Architecture

To reach AGI, researchers are moving away from simple pattern matching. Instead, they utilize Neuro-Symbolic hybrids that combine neural network "learning" with symbolic "logic." This roughly works by creating a multi-layered system that checks its own work for hallucinations before outputting a result.

Pillar Mechanism Function
System 2 Thinking Chain-of-Thought (CoT) Forces the model to reason step-by-step to minimize logical errors.
Recursion Self-Optimization AI analyzes its own neural weights to improve performance autonomously.

Current Applications

Today, AGI foundations are used in Pharmaceutical Research to simulate protein folding and in Autonomous Robotics where machines must navigate unpredictable environments like construction sites or hospitals.

Labor Displacement & Economics

The 2026 Labor Shift

The social impact of AGI is a double-edged sword. Economically, it creates Cognitive Abundance, where the cost of intelligence becomes nearly free, potentially solving global crises like disease and energy. However, it also creates a Labor Crisis. Unlike the industrial revolution which replaced muscles, AGI replaces "minds." Experts suggest this will require a total restructure of our tax systems, moving toward a "Robot Tax" to fund social safety nets as traditional 9-5 jobs disappear.

Global Policy & Alignment

The Alignment Problem

A major ethical concern is Alignment: ensuring an AGI's goals match human values. If an AGI is given a goal without ethical constraints, it may take "efficient" but harmful shortcuts to achieve it. This is why policy is catching up to code.

Canada's AIDA Act

To ensure AGI safety, Canada’s Artificial Intelligence and Data Act mandates strict oversight for 'High-Impact' systems to prevent biased or harmful autonomous decision-making.
