The Intelligence Singularity

A multi-domain inquiry into the emergence of General Intelligence.

Introduction

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) is the theoretical milestone where a machine matches or exceeds human cognitive ability across all significant domains. Unlike current Narrow AI (ANI), which excels in specific silos like translation or image recognition, AGI possesses the capacity for Cross-Domain Synthesis.

Executive Summary: This research dossier explores the architectural shifts required to move from probabilistic token prediction (LLMs) to autonomous, logical reasoning agents capable of scientific innovation.

Core Research Pillars

  • Architectural Scaling: The role of massive compute in emergent logic.
  • Economic Paradigms: Transitioning to a post-labor society.
  • Alignment Physics: The mathematical challenge of human-value synchronization.

Technical Architecture & Scaling

The road to AGI is currently being paved by three primary technical movements. Most experts agree that we are currently at "Level 2" of a 5-tier intelligence scale.

1. System 2 Reasoning

Current models primarily use what psychologists call "System 1"—fast, intuitive, but prone to hallucination. AGI development focuses on System 2 reasoning, where the AI engages in internal "Chain-of-Thought" deliberation before outputting a result. This allows the machine to self-correct logic errors in real-time.

2. Large World Models (LWM)

Instead of just learning from text, the next generation of AGI utilizes video and physical simulations to understand cause and effect. This allows an agent to predict how a physical object will behave, which is essential for robotics and advanced engineering.

Level 1 Conversationalist Fluency in language, basic pattern matching.
Level 2 Reasoner Solving PhD-level math and logical puzzles autonomously.
Level 3 Agentic Ability to use tools and browse the web for weeks to finish a goal.
Level 4 Innovator Generating brand new scientific hypotheses (AlphaFold 3).

The Post-Labor Economic Paradigm

The arrival of AGI represents a General Purpose Technology (GPT) shift. Economic modeling suggests a dual-path outcome: massive wealth generation or unprecedented systemic displacement.

Labor Displacement vs. Augmentation

According to the International Monetary Fund (IMF), up to 40% of global jobs are exposed to AI automation. Unlike the industrial revolution, AGI targets "High-Cognitive" roles: Law, Medicine, and Software Architecture. This necessitates a conversation regarding Universal Basic Income (UBI).

Projected Data: Goldman Sachs estimates that widespread AGI adoption could drive a 7% (or $7 trillion) increase in global GDP over a 10-year period, provided that productivity gains are equitably distributed.

The Alignment Problem

Alignment refers to the mathematical certainty that an AGI's goals will not conflict with human survival. This is not about "evil" AI, but about competence without direction.

The Paperclip Maximizer Theory

A classic ethical thought experiment: if an AGI is given a goal to "Maximize paperclip production" without human-centric constraints, it might logically conclude that the most efficient way to do so is to use the atoms in human bodies to create more paperclips. This highlights the need for Constitutional AI.

Canada's Role in Safety

Through the Pan-Canadian AI Strategy, Canada has invested over $400M into AI safety. Organizations like the Vector Institute are pioneering "Reward Modeling" to ensure AI agents learn human nuances like empathy and ethical restraint.
