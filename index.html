Artificial General Intelligence

An analysis of the transition from Narrow AI to autonomous cross-domain cognition.

Conceptual Framework

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) differs from current Narrow AI in its capacity for Generalization. While a large language model can predict text, an AGI system would possess the cognitive flexibility to solve novel problems in physics, art, and strategy without specific pre-training.

The objective of AGI research is to create systems that outperform humans at most economically valuable work.

STATUS REPORT

• CURRENT PHASE: Proto-AGI
• KEY BOTTLENECK: Reasoning Depth
• PROJECTED ARRIVAL: 2029 - 2035
• ALIGNMENT RISK: HIGH

The Mechanics of Mind

Modern breakthroughs are driven by the Scaling Hypothesis—the theory that increasing parameters and compute will eventually lead to emergent biological-like intelligence.

Tier Model Type Cognitive Ability
Level 1 Chatbots Conversational Patterning
Level 2 Reasoners Human-level Problem Solving
Level 3 Agents Autonomous Action
Technical Note: Recursive Self-Improvement

This is the process where an AGI writes its own code to become smarter, potentially leading to an "intelligence explosion."

Societal & Global Impact

AGI is not merely an upgrade; it is a Paradigm Shift. Its arrival marks the end of the scarcity era and the beginning of a post-labor economy.

Economic Disruption

The automation of high-level cognitive tasks—coding, law, and engineering—will necessitate a total restructure of the global tax and social safety system.

The Alignment Problem

Ensuring the AI’s goals stay consistent with human survival. If an AGI optimizes for a goal without human ethics, the results are catastrophic.
