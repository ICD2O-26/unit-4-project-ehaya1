Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)

What is AGI?

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) is a type of artificial intelligence that can think, learn, and solve problems like a human. Unlike regular AI, which is designed for one task, AGI can work across many different areas without needing retraining.

How Does AGI Work?

AGI systems are built using neural networks, which are computer systems that learn from large amounts of data. These systems recognize patterns and improve over time.

More advanced AI uses step-by-step reasoning, sometimes called "chain-of-thought," to solve complex problems more accurately.

Where is AGI Used?

True AGI does not fully exist yet, but similar technologies are used in:

  • Healthcare (diagnosing diseases)
  • Self-driving cars
  • Customer service chatbots
  • Software development

Social Impacts of AGI

Positive Impacts

AGI could improve healthcare, automate dangerous jobs, and make everyday life easier by solving complex problems quickly.

Negative Impacts

AGI could replace many jobs, especially in fields like law, coding, and customer service. This may lead to unemployment and economic challenges.

Ethical Concerns

One major issue is making sure AGI follows human values. If not properly controlled, it could make harmful decisions.
