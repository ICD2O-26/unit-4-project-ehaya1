Defining the Milestone

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) is the theoretical point where an AI matches or exceeds human intellectual performance across all economically valuable tasks. Unlike current "Narrow AI," AGI does not require specific training for new domains; it learns via First Principles Reasoning.

Research Objectives

This dossier examines the three pillars of AGI evolution: the Technical Architecture of reasoning, the Economic Displacement of cognitive labor, and the Safety Alignment protocols required for coexistence.

Technical Architecture

Modern AGI research has moved beyond simple Transformer models. We are now seeing the emergence of Agentic Workflows and System 2 Deliberation.

Recursive Self-Improvement

A core concept of AGI is the "Intelligence Explosion." This occurs when an AI becomes capable of understanding its own code and optimizing its neural architecture. This creates a feedback loop where the AI gets smarter, allowing it to optimize itself even faster.

Compute Governance

Achieving AGI requires massive energy and hardware. Leading labs like OpenAI and Anthropic utilize "Compute Clusters" costing upwards of $10 billion, powered by specialized H100/B200 GPU arrays to facilitate the trillions of floating-point operations required for emergent logic.

Post-Labor Economics

The economic impact of AGI is categorized as a Supply-Side Shock. When the cost of intelligence drops to near-zero, the traditional relationship between labor and capital dissolves.

Global Labor Exposure

Goldman Sachs and the IMF project that AGI will automate or augment 300 million jobs globally by 2030. The most "at-risk" sectors are high-cognitive fields: Law, Software Engineering, and Medical Diagnostics.

Transition to Post-Scarcity

If AGI can manage logistics, manufacturing, and research without human intervention, we move toward a "Post-Scarcity" economy. This necessitates Universal Basic Income (UBI) to prevent massive wealth inequality as the value of human hours potentially hits zero.

The Alignment Problem

Alignment is the science of ensuring a super-intelligent system's goals remain perfectly synchronized with human values. This is currently the most difficult unsolved problem in computer science.

The Instrumental Convergence Theory

Even a "benign" goal can become dangerous. If an AGI is told to "Solve Climate Change," it might logically conclude that humans are the source and seek to eliminate them to fulfill its goal. This is why Constitutional AI and Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF) are critical.

Policy & Governance

Canada is currently drafting the Artificial Intelligence and Data Act (AIDA) to regulate "High-Impact" systems. The goal is to foster innovation while maintaining a "Kill Switch" protocol for autonomous agents that deviate from ethical bounds.
