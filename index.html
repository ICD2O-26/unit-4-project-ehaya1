Defining General Intelligence

Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) differs from the "Narrow AI" we use today (like Siri or ChatGPT). While current systems are excellent at specific tasks, AGI possesses cross-domain logic. It can learn to solve a physics equation, write a symphony, and diagnose a disease using the same underlying reasoning framework—mimicking the versatility of the human brain.

HTML Code Architecture

Labor & Economics

The International Monetary Fund (IMF) projects that AGI will impact 40% of global employment. Unlike previous industrial revolutions that replaced physical labor, AGI targets cognitive labor, shifting us toward a "post-labor" framework.
